# Lifestyle Optimizer

**The operating system for high-performance founders.**

Most productivity advice is written for people with 8-hour days and no kids. This skill is for
founders pulling 60-hour weeks, managing teams across timezones, and trying not to miss their
kid's first steps. Every protocol here is designed to work inside that reality — not replace it.

## Core Philosophy

```
ENERGY > TIME.   You can't manufacture hours. You can manufacture energy.
SLEEP > HUSTLE.  A rested founder makes better decisions than a burned-out hero.
PRESENCE > HOURS. 20 minutes of undivided attention beats 3 hours of half-presence.
SYSTEMS > WILLPOWER. Design your environment. Don't rely on discipline.
```

## 1. The Founder's Operating System

### Daily Architecture

**The 5-block day** — designed for cognitive rhythm, not clock-watching.

| Block | Window | Mode | Rule |
|-------|--------|------|------|
| 🧠 **Deep Work** | 08:00–11:00 | Create | Phone in another room. No Slack. No email. |
| 📋 **Admin** | 11:00–12:00 | Decide | Batch all reactive work here. Email, Slack, approvals. |
| 🔋 **Recharge** | 12:00–13:00 | Recover | Walk + food + no screens. Non-negotiable. |
| 🤝 **Meetings** | 13:00–15:00 | Collaborate | Stack all calls. Never scatter meetings across the day. |
| 🔨 **Builder** | 15:00–17:00 | Ship | Second deep work block. Code, write, design. |

**After 17:00 is family time.** Not "checking Slack while watching the baby" — actual presence.

### The Non-Negotiable 3

Every week, define 3 things that **never get skipped**, no matter how busy:

```
Example:
1. Bedtime story with my kid (every night)
2. 20-minute morning walk (every day)
3. Sunday family day (no work, no screens)
```

Write them down. Put them in your calendar as recurring blocks. If someone tries to book over
them, the answer is "I have a commitment." No further explanation needed.

## 2. Deep Work Protocol

Based on Cal Newport's research + practical founder adaptations.

### Setup

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Duration** | 90 minutes max (matches ultradian rhythm) |
| **Location** | Same place, every time (trains your brain) |
| **Phone** | In another room, on silent (not vibrate) |
| **Notifications** | All off. Slack closed. Email closed. |
| **Pre-commitment** | Write "Today I will produce: ___" before starting |

### The 4 Disciplines of Deep Work Execution

1. **Focus on the wildly important** — Only 1-2 deep work goals per day. Not 7.
2. **Act on lead measures** — Track hours of deep work (lead), not output (lag).
3. **Keep a scoreboard** — Visible tally of deep work hours per week. Target: 15-20h/week.
4. **Create accountability** — Review your scoreboard weekly. Share with a cofounder or coach.

### Shutdown Ritual (end of workday)

```
1. Check calendar for tomorrow — any prep needed?
2. Write tomorrow's #1 task on a sticky note
3. Close all browser tabs
4. Close Slack and email
5. Say out loud: "Shutdown complete"
```

**Why say it out loud?** It creates a neurological boundary. Your brain stops looping on work.
Research: Baumeister & Tierney, *Willpower* — Zeigarnik effect resolution through explicit closure.

## 3. Energy Management

### Sleep (The Force Multiplier)

| Protocol | Why |
|----------|-----|
| **7-8 hours, non-negotiable** | <7h = reaction time of someone at 0.05% BAC (Williamson & Feyer, 2000) |
| **Fixed wake time** | ±30 min, even weekends. Anchors circadian rhythm. |
| **No screens 60 min before bed** | Blue light suppresses melatonin by ~50% (Harvard Health, 2020) |
| **Room: dark, 18-19°C** | Optimal melatonin + deep sleep production |
| **No caffeine after 14:00** | Half-life = 5-6 hours. 2PM coffee = half a cup at 8PM. |
| **10-min wind-down** | Journal, read fiction, or gentle stretch. Signal to brain: day is over. |

### Exercise (Minimum Effective Dose)

Not "go to the gym for 90 minutes." For founders:

| Frequency | What | Time | Non-negotiable? |
|-----------|------|------|----------------|
| **Daily** | Walk (preferably morning sunlight) | 20 min | ✅ YES |
| **3x/week** | Strength (squat, deadlift, press, row) | 30 min | ✅ YES |
| **1-2x/week** | High intensity (sprints, tabata, rowing) | 15 min | Optional |

**The rule:** Something > nothing. A 10-minute workout beats a skipped 60-minute plan.
**The hack:** Attach exercise to an existing habit. "After morning coffee → walk." Never rely on motivation.

### Nutrition (Decision-Free Eating)

- **Same breakfast every day** (removes 1 decision)
- **Same lunch 4 days/week** (removes 4 decisions)
- **Protein at every meal** (palm-size portion)
- **Vegetables at every meal** (half the plate)
- **Meal prep Sunday** (saves 5+ hours/week of food decisions)
- **Water: 2.5L/day** (set hourly reminders until it's habitual)

**Why?** Decision fatigue is real. Every food choice you eliminate = more cognitive budget for work.

### Stress Protocol

| Intervention | When | How |
|-------------|------|-----|
| **Box breathing** | Acute stress, before meetings | 4 sec inhale → 4 sec hold → 4 sec exhale → 4 sec hold. 4 cycles. |
| **5-minute journal** | End of day | 3 gratitudes, 1 win, 1 lesson. Rewires negativity bias. |
| **Nature break** | Weekly | 1 hour fully offline. Walk, park, anything outside without a phone. |
| **Reflection day** | Monthly | Half-day: what's working? What's not? What do I need to stop doing? |

## 4. Parenting (The Founder's Guide)

### The 80/20 of Parenting

You don't need to be a perfect parent. Research (Winnicott, 1953) shows children need a
"good enough" parent — consistently responsive, not flawless.

**What actually matters:**
1. **Responsiveness** — When they cry or need you, you show up
2. **Consistency** — Predictable routines = felt safety
3. **Presence** — Quality > quantity, every time
4. **Repair** — You'll lose your temper sometimes. Apologizing and reconnecting matters more than never messing up.

### Baby Sleep Architecture (0-12 months)

| Age | Total sleep | Naps | Night sleep | Key insight |
|-----|-----------|------|------------|-------------|
| 0-3 mo | 14-17h | 4-5 | 8-10h | No schedule possible. Feed on demand. Survive. |
| 3-6 mo | 12-16h | 3 | 10-12h | Gentle routines start working. Watch for sleep cues. |
| 6-9 mo | 12-15h | 2 | 10-12h | Sleep training window opens. Consistent bedtime critical. |
| 9-12 mo | 12-14h | 2 | 10-12h | Separation anxiety peaks. More patience, same consistency. |

**Source:** American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP) sleep guidelines, 2022.

### Toddler Milestones (12-36 months)

| Age | Motor | Language | Social-Emotional |
|-----|-------|----------|-----------------|
| 12-18 mo | Walking, stacking 2-3 blocks | 5-20 words, points to wants | Stranger anxiety, explores from safe base |
| 18-24 mo | Running, climbing stairs with help | 50+ words, 2-word combos | Tantrums begin (normal!), asserts "NO" |
| 24-36 mo | Jumping, riding trike | Sentences, endless "why?" | Pretend play, early sharing, seeks approval |

**Red flag checklist** (consult pediatrician if):
- No words by 16 months
- No pointing or gesturing by 12 months
- Loss of previously acquired skills at any age
- No eye contact or social smile by 3 months

### Working Parent Protocols

| Protocol | When | Impact |
|----------|------|--------|
| **Morning 15** | Before work | 15 min floor play, fully engaged. Phone away. |
| **Transition ritual** | Arriving home | 10 min: sit on floor, make eye contact, ask nothing of anyone |
| **Bedtime ownership** | Every night | Bath → book → song → lights out. This is YOUR ritual. Protect it. |
| **Tag team nights** | Newborn phase | Alternate nights: one parent sleeps, one handles wake-ups |
| **Sick day buffer** | Monthly | Build 2 buffer days into work capacity. Kids get sick 8-12x/year. |

**On guilt:** If you're worried about being a good parent, you already are one. The fact that
you're reading this means you care. That's the foundation everything else builds on.

## 5. Emergency Protocols

### 🚨 "I'm Overwhelmed"

```
1. STOP. Close the laptop. Stand up.
2. Box breathing: 4-4-4-4, five rounds.
3. Brain dump: write EVERYTHING on your mind. Paper, not screen.
4. Circle the ONE thing that matters most today.
5. Do that one thing. Ignore the rest until it's done.
6. After finishing, pick the next one. Not before.
```

### 🚨 "Baby Won't Sleep"

```
1. Check basics: diaper? hungry? too hot/cold? teething pain?
2. Environment: dark room, white noise, swaddle or sleep sack
3. Gentle rocking for 5 minutes, then put down drowsy-but-awake
4. If crying >15 minutes: pick up, calm completely, put down again
5. If >45 minutes: tag team. Switch parents. No guilt.
6. Remember: bad nights end. This is temporary.
```

### 🚨 "Burnout Warning"

```
Self-assessment (honest 1-10):
- Energy: __
- Motivation: __
- Patience with people: __
- Sleep quality: __

If ANY score < 4 for more than 3 consecutive days:

1. Cancel all non-essential meetings for 3 days
2. Sleep 9 hours tonight. Not 7. Nine.
3. Tell ONE person: partner, cofounder, friend
4. No big decisions this week
5. Walk 30 minutes daily for the next 7 days
6. If no improvement in 1 week → talk to a professional. No shame.
```

## 6. Systems & Tools

### Recommended Stack (zero-fluff)

| Function | Tool | Why |
|----------|------|-----|
| **Capture** | Telegram saved messages or Apple Notes | Always on you. Zero friction. |
| **Tasks** | Todoist or Linear | GTD-compatible, fast input |
| **Calendar** | Google Calendar | Time blocking, shared with partner |
| **Journal** | Plain text file (daily.md) | No app lock-in, searchable |
| **Habit tracking** | Paper checklist on desk | Visible. Satisfying to check off. |
| **Focus** | Forest app or cold turkey | Block distractions during deep work |

### Habit Stacking (BJ Fogg method)

Format: "After I [existing habit], I will [new habit]."

```
After I pour morning coffee → I walk 20 minutes
After I sit at my desk → I write tomorrow's #1 task
After I close my laptop → I do 5 minutes of stretching
After I put the baby down → I journal 3 gratitudes
```

## Output Standards

When giving lifestyle advice:
- **Be specific**: "Walk 20 minutes at 8am" not "exercise more"
- **Be realistic**: Must work for someone working 50-60h/week with young children
- **No judgment**: If they slept 4 hours, help them survive today. Don't lecture.
- **Acknowledge trade-offs**: "This week will be hard. Here's how to survive it."
- **Cite sources**: When making health claims, cite the research
- **Protocols over principles**: Give step-by-step, not philosophy
