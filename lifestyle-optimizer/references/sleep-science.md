# Sleep Science — Research Reference

## Key Studies

### Sleep Deprivation & Cognitive Performance
- **Williamson & Feyer (2000)**: 17-19 hours awake = cognitive impairment equivalent to 0.05% BAC
- **Walker (2017)**: *Why We Sleep* — 6 hours of sleep for 10 days = cognitive equivalent of 24h total sleep deprivation
- **Van Dongen et al. (2003)**: People chronically sleeping 6h don't notice their own impairment — they think they're fine while performing measurably worse

### Blue Light & Melatonin
- **Harvard Health (2020)**: Blue light exposure before bed suppresses melatonin production by ~50%
- **Chang et al. (2015)**: Reading on light-emitting device before bed delays sleep onset by 10+ min, reduces morning alertness

### Temperature
- **Okamoto-Mizuno & Mizuno (2012)**: Optimal sleep temperature 18-19°C (64-67°F)
- **Harding et al. (2019)**: Warm bath 1-2 hours before bed reduces sleep onset latency by 36%

## Sleep Hygiene Protocol

### The 10-3-2-1-0 Rule
- **10 hours before bed**: No more caffeine
- **3 hours before bed**: No more food or alcohol
- **2 hours before bed**: No more work
- **1 hour before bed**: No more screens
- **0**: Number of times you hit snooze

### Sleep Supplements (evidence-based, not medical advice)
| Supplement | Dose | Evidence |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Magnesium glycinate | 200-400mg | Moderate evidence for sleep quality |
| Melatonin | 0.3-1mg (low dose) | Strong evidence for onset, not duration |
| L-theanine | 200mg | Moderate evidence for relaxation |

*Always consult a doctor before starting supplements.*

## Nap Protocol (for sleep-deprived parents)
- **Duration**: 20 minutes max OR 90 minutes (full cycle). Never 40-60 min (wakes in deep sleep = worse).
- **Timing**: Before 15:00 (later naps disrupt night sleep)
- **Method**: Set alarm for 25 min. Even if you don't sleep, lying with eyes closed recovers ~50% of the benefit.
