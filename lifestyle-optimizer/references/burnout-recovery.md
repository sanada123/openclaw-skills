# Burnout Recovery Protocol

Based on Maslach Burnout Inventory (MBI) and recovery research.

## The 3 Dimensions of Burnout (Maslach & Leiter)

1. **Emotional Exhaustion** — "I have nothing left to give"
2. **Depersonalization** — "I don't care about this anymore"  
3. **Reduced Accomplishment** — "Nothing I do matters"

If you recognize 2 out of 3 for more than 2 weeks → you're burned out, not just tired.

## Self-Assessment

Rate 1-10 (be honest):

```
Emotional energy:     __/10
Motivation for work:  __/10
Patience with people: __/10
Sleep quality:        __/10
Physical energy:      __/10
Joy outside of work:  __/10
```

**Score interpretation:**
- 48-60: Healthy. Maintain current habits.
- 36-47: Warning zone. Make changes this week.
- 24-35: Approaching burnout. Take action today.
- Below 24: Burnout. Professional support recommended.

## Recovery Protocol

### Phase 1: Emergency Stabilization (Days 1-3)

- Cancel ALL non-essential commitments
- Sleep 9 hours minimum
- No decisions that can wait
- Walk 30 minutes daily
- Tell one trusted person how you feel
- Eat regular meals (even if no appetite)

### Phase 2: Strategic Recovery (Days 4-14)

- Identify the top 3 energy drains (tasks, people, situations)
- Delegate or eliminate at least 1 of them
- Reintroduce one enjoyable non-work activity
- Limit work to 6 focused hours/day
- Daily: 10 minutes journaling ("What do I actually WANT?")
- No alcohol (it makes exhaustion worse, not better)

### Phase 3: Rebuild (Days 15-30)

- Redesign your week around energy, not tasks
- Set hard boundaries (define "available" hours, communicate them)
- Resume exercise (gentle: walks, stretching, yoga)
- Create a "not-doing" list (things you commit to STOP doing)
- Schedule a weekly check-in with yourself (Friday review)

### Phase 4: Prevention (Ongoing)

- Monthly burnout self-assessment (the 6-question score above)
- Quarterly "life audit" — am I aligned with what matters?
- Annual: at least 1 week completely disconnected from work
- Build "recovery days" into your calendar proactively (not reactively)

## When to Seek Professional Help

- Self-assessment below 24 for more than 2 weeks
- Persistent sleep problems (falling asleep, staying asleep)
- Loss of interest in things you used to enjoy
- Physical symptoms (headaches, chest tightness, stomach issues) without medical cause
- Thoughts of harming yourself → immediate help: call a crisis line

**There is no weakness in asking for help. The strongest founders are the ones who know when
they need support.**
